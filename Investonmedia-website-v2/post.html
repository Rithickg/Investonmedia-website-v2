what is a Mutual Fund ? Is there risk in investing in a Mutual Fund ? Things to keep in mind before investing in a Mutual Fund

what is a Mutual Fund ?


A mutual fund is an investment fund managed by professionals where they invest the money to purchase stocks and securities for the investors and return the profit to the investors. The fund managing company or manager gets a certain amount of money in the form of an expanse ratio for managing the funds.


Is there risk in investing in a Mutual Fund ?

There is always risk in investing in both stocks and mutual funds. The mutual fund also has its own risk. If the fund is managed poorly by the company and fund manager then there is a possible risk of losing the money over a period of time. It is important to check the performance of the fund in the past years to understand the strength of the fund. Another important thing to keep in mind is to check where the funds are being invested - sectors.


Things to keep in mind before investing in a Mutual Fund

Expanse Ratio

The expanse Ratio is the important thing to consider before investing in a fund because a portion of the money is given to the fund manager or company for managing the fund. The higher expanse ratio can take away a huge amount of the profit for the long term made by the fund. This can be avoided by investing in lower expanse ratio funds.

An expanse Ratio of less than 1% is considered to be a good option. An Expanse Ratio above 1.5% is considered to be a bad investment.


Lock-in period

Some funds have a lock-in period in which you cannot withdraw cash till the lock-in period is over.


Exit-load

Exit load is a particular amount to be paid when you exit the fund within a certain period. This method is used to make the investors stay on the fund for a longer run.
